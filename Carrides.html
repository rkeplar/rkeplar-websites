Car Rides!

Each and every time I take Daisy outside, she makes a beeline to the car. She takes off at full speed and makes me sprint after her clutching the leash! She will run in the grass along the line of cars until she sniffs our car and will wait patiently - or not so patiently- at the door. It's like she found the prize at the bottom of the cereal box, and if that day isn't a car ride day but a walk-around-the -neighborhood day, she will trot away for the walk very proud that she found the car.

But car ride days are always her favorite days.

She loves to stick her head out the window and feel the air, and on busier streets she likes to take turns watching out every window to get the best angle from which to watch the city sweep by.

She knows the car ride will take her to somewhere new to explore. A place yet unsniffed. Will it be the nature preserve? The quiet neighborhood park? A trip to Mom and Dad's? Road trip? Sometimes I'll take her along for the drive to the store, and while she would rather come inside the store with me, she is always happy to see me come back with treats and her favorite snacks.

Daisy reminds me to get excited about the little things in life. She reminds me to drive with my windows down and feel the breeze every so often. To get excited that I have another day where I get to drive, get to walk, get to explore. To stop and appreciate the life and its beauty.

This semester has been crazy. Working a full time job as well as being a research assistant and gaining an internship at a place I've always aspired to work at - it feels like I have no time to myself to relax. But taking a moment to breathe, to feel alive, to watch the sun set and sun rise, there is beauty in every thing even if it is a routine. Perhaps even more so if it is routine as you can always count on it to be there. It is the little moments that help keep people grounded and motivated to also keep going no matter how tough things may seem.
